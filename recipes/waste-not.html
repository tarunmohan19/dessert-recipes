Waste Not Cake Recipe

Description

This cake takes advantage of ingredients that normally would be thrown out and creates an amazing dessert out of it. Perfect for the Depression Era Family for which to waste ingredients simply was not an option. It's an awesome way to create a great food with the ingredients you have lying around.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch ground cloves (Optional)
  • 1 2/3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup soured milk
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon boiling water

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (180 degrees C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Cream sugar and butter together in a large bowl. Beat in eggs, cocoa powder, cinnamon, and cloves. Add flour alternately with soured milk, stirring batter until smooth. Fold in raisins. Dissolve baking soda in boiling water and add to the batter. Pour batter into the prepared baking dish.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean, 30 to 35 minutes. Cool before slicing.
