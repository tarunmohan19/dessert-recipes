Water Pie Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 5 tbsp. butter, divided into parts
  • A pie crust, either store-bought or homemade

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 400 degrees F (or 204 degrees C).
  2. If you're using a store-bought pie crust, roll it into a regular nine-inch pie plate. Crimp or trim the edges of excess dough. If you're making a pie crust, follow directions of the recipe. Then, roll the crust into a nine-inch pie plate. Crimp or trim the edges.
  3. Pour the water into the pie crust.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the flour and sugar. Whisk until well mixed. Sprinkle flour mixture into the water. Do not stir.
  5. Drizzle the vanilla extract into the pie. Place the pats of butter at the top of the mixture so they float.
  6. Bake at 400 degrees F for 30 minutes. Then reduce the heat to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C) for 25-30 minutes. Remove from oven and let cool.
